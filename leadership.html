BOW Leadership

BOW creates an environment where its members can thrive.

In our community, our members are given tools to develop as leaders, businesswomen, and individuals. Each year, several of our members serve on BOW's Executive Board to plan workshops, invite guest speakers to campus, form corporate sponsorships, carry out logistics, and more. The Executive Board strives to create the ultimate experience for Duke women interested in business by creating programming across all industries and skill sets.

High School Mentorship

VP of Professional Development: Hayden Mansaeu


We are looking for BOW women to join the High School Mentorship Committee who are passionate about helping younger women and excited about building a brand-new BOW initiative. Committee members will have the opportunity to help shape this new program by taking ownership over the planning of 1-2 mentorship sessions per year. You will have the chance to build an engaging lesson plan and present on topics relevant to young women interested in business including Entrepreneurship in the Triangle, Public Speaking, Owning your Accomplishments, and Resume Building. No experience is required—we are looking for BOW members of all ages and backgrounds with strong organizational and public speaking skills who want to be more involved in BOW.
